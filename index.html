The Town Chronicles

Danyel Robinson aka Dru Down – Oakland’s finest, no alibi. Born and raised in East Oakland, Dru Down is a cornerstone of the Bay Area’s gangsta rap scene, blending 90s grit with timeless funk. Son of funk legend Bootsy Collins, Dru’s legacy bridges street culture and musical innovation.

  • Pioneered the Bay Sound with G-funk beats
  • Influenced Mac Dre, E-40, and the hyphy movement
  • Founder of Pimp On Records
  • Active in music, film, and community
  • Starred as Kayo in the cult classic film Original Gangstas (1996), a pivotal moment connecting Bay Area hip-hop to national cinema. Watch on Tubi

Raised by his grandmother and stepfather after his mother’s passing, Dru Down’s journey is one of resilience and authenticity. His discovery that funk legend Bootsy Collins is his biological father further shaped his musical identity, blending P-Funk legacy with Oakland street wisdom. Despite legal struggles and time behind bars, Dru Down’s music and story continue to inspire, making him a true independent legend of the Bay.
